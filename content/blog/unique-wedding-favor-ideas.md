Wedding favors are one of those details that can feel like an afterthought — but they really don't have to be. The right favor is something your guests will actually use, keep, or talk about long after your big day. Forget the generic Jordan almonds and miniature picture frames nobody asked for. These are the wedding favor ideas that people genuinely love.

We've rounded up our favourite unique wedding favors, complete with where to get them, so you can skip the endless scrolling and go straight to the good stuff.

## 1. Coffee Beans

<div class="blog-photo-grid">
<img src="/images/blog-favors-coffee-1.webp" alt="Personalised coffee bean wedding favors in kraft bags with The Perfect Blend label" />
<img src="/images/blog-favors-coffee-2.jpg" alt="Custom coffee bag wedding favors displayed in rustic wooden crates with greenery" />
</div>
<small>Photos via <a href="https://pinterest.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pinterest</a></small>

If you and your partner are the kind of couple who can't function before your morning coffee, this one's a no-brainer. Personalised coffee bean favors are practical, stylish, and guaranteed to actually get used — which is more than you can say for most wedding favors. Pack them into kraft paper bags with a custom label featuring your names and date, or go all out with a "The Perfect Blend" pun that's cheesy in the best possible way.

You can source beans from a local roaster to make it extra special, or order pre-packaged options from Etsy sellers who handle the labels and bags for you. Either way, your guests get a genuinely useful keepsake and you get the satisfaction of knowing your favors won't end up in the bin.

## 2. Personalised Sketches or Paintings

<div class="blog-photo-grid">
<img src="/images/blog-favors-sketches-1.webp" alt="Watercolour painting of couple as personalised wedding favor" />
<img src="/images/blog-favors-sketches-2.jpg" alt="Collection of personalised watercolour guest portraits as wedding favors" />
</div>
<small>Photos via <a href="https://pinterest.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pinterest</a></small>

This is hands down one of the most memorable wedding favor ideas you'll ever come across. Hire a live sketch artist or caricaturist to draw guests during the reception, and they leave with a one-of-a-kind portrait they'll actually want to frame. It doubles as entertainment and a favor — two birds, one brilliantly talented stone.

If a live artist isn't in the budget, you can also commission pre-made watercolour prints of your venue, a meaningful landmark, or a custom illustration that ties into your wedding theme. Guests pick one up from a display table on their way out, and it feels far more personal than anything mass-produced ever could.

## 3. Herb Pots

<div class="blog-photo-grid">
<img src="/images/blog-favors-herbs-1.jpg" alt="Mini herb pots with personalised initials as wedding favors" />
<img src="/images/blog-favors-herbs-2.jpg" alt="Olive branch plant wedding favor wrapped in linen with wax seal" />
</div>
<small>Photos via <a href="https://pinterest.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pinterest</a></small>

There's something lovely about giving your guests a living, growing gift. Mini herb pots — think rosemary, lavender, basil, or thyme — are a gorgeous wedding favor that doubles as table decor during the reception and a kitchen companion for months afterwards. Pop them in small terracotta pots with a tag that reads something like "Let Love Grow" and they look effortlessly elegant on any table setting.

They work particularly well for garden weddings, rustic venues, or any couple who loves to cook together. You can source small potted herbs from local nurseries in bulk, or order ready-to-go sets from Etsy with custom labels already attached.

<tr><th>What we love most</th></tr>

<tr><td>Doubles as stunning table decor during the reception</td></tr>

</table>

## 4. Scarves and Shawls

<div class="blog-photo-grid">
<img src="/images/blog-favors-scarves-1.jpg" alt="Grey pashmina shawl wedding favors with personalised Thank You labels" />
<img src="/images/blog-favors-scarves-2.jpg" alt="Basket of cream shawl wedding favors with ribbon and monogram tags" />
</div>
<small>Photos via <a href="https://pinterest.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pinterest</a></small>

If you're planning an outdoor or autumn wedding, pashminas and lightweight scarves are a wedding favor that your guests will genuinely thank you for. There's always that moment in the evening when the temperature drops and everyone wishes they'd brought a jacket — these solve that problem beautifully while also serving as a keepsake they'll actually wear again.

Roll them up with a ribbon and a tag, drape them over each chair, or display them in a basket near the entrance with a sign that says "To Have and To Hold (When You Get Cold)." You can find bulk pashminas in dozens of colours on Amazon, Etsy, or directly from wholesale suppliers — often for less than you'd expect.

<div class="blog-photo-grid">
<img src="/images/blog-favors-lottery-1.jpg" alt="Lottery scratch card wedding favor in personalised kraft bag with Life is a Lottery quote" />
<img src="/images/blog-favors-lottery-2.webp" alt="Lucky in Love scratch card wedding favor envelopes with coins" />
</div>
<small>Photos via <a href="https://pinterest.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pinterest</a></small>

This is one of those wedding favor ideas that gets the whole table buzzing. Place a scratch card at each setting and suddenly your guests have something fun to do between courses — and a genuine chance of leaving your wedding richer than they arrived. It adds a little thrill to the evening and always gets people talking.

You can use real lottery scratch cards from your local shop, or order custom-designed ones with your names and wedding date on them. Some couples even create their own "scratch to reveal" cards with personalised prizes like a bottle of wine, a late-night snack, or the last slice of cake.

## 6. Custom Card Decks

<div class="blog-photo-grid">
<img src="/images/blog-favors-cards-1.jpg" alt="Custom illustrated playing card deck wedding favor with watercolour designs" />
<img src="/images/blog-favors-cards-2.webp" alt="Personalised illustrated playing cards with wedding invitation" />
</div>
<small>Photos via <a href="https://pinterest.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pinterest</a></small>

A custom deck of playing cards is one of those wedding favors that guests will reach for again and again — on holiday, at dinner parties, on lazy weekends at home. Every time they shuffle the deck, they'll think of your wedding day. Commission an illustrator to create a bespoke design featuring your portraits, your pets, your venue, or details from your wedding theme, and you've got a truly one-of-a-kind keepsake that's as beautiful as it is fun.

The best part is that playing cards are timeless. They don't expire, they don't wilt, and they don't collect dust on a shelf. They get used, passed around, and enjoyed — which is exactly what a great wedding favor should do. You can find custom illustrated decks through Etsy artists or specialist printers who work with couples to bring their story to life on every card.

<div class="blog-photo-grid">
<img src="/images/blog-favors-charity-1.jpg" alt="Charity donation wedding favor card on gold charger plate in lieu of a favor" />

If you and your partner feel strongly about a cause, making a charitable donation on behalf of your guests is one of the most meaningful wedding favors you can give. Place a beautifully designed card at each setting explaining that a donation has been made in their name — it's elegant, generous, and says a lot about who you are as a couple.

Choose a charity that's personal to you, whether it's a cause you've supported together, something close to your family, or an organisation that reflects your values. Many charities offer specific wedding donation packages with branded cards and pins that tie in beautifully with your table decor.

<img src="/images/blog-favors-compass-1.jpg" alt="Gold compass wedding favors with calligraphy escort cards inside" />

For the couple who loves to travel — or simply wants a favor with real meaning — a miniature compass is a beautiful choice. It carries that lovely symbolism of finding your way together, and it's the kind of object that guests will keep on a desk or bookshelf for years. Gold or brass compasses look particularly stunning and photograph beautifully on the table.

They also double brilliantly as escort cards — have each guest's name and table number calligraphed inside the lid, and you've combined your seating plan with your favor in one elegant move. You can find engraved compass favors in bulk on Etsy and Amazon, often personalised with your initials or wedding date.

<div class="blog-photo-grid">

Whether you go the wellness route with mini essential oil bottles or the foodie route with herb-infused olive oils, this is a wedding favor that feels luxurious without the luxury price tag. Essential oils like lavender or eucalyptus come in beautiful little glass bottles that look stunning on a table, while infused oils — think rosemary, chilli, or garlic — are perfect for couples who love to cook and want to share that passion.

You can make infused oils yourself for a truly homemade touch, or source them from artisan producers and small businesses. Add a personalised label with your names and date, and they feel like a boutique gift rather than a mass-produced favor.

Nothing says "we put thought into this" quite like a homemade wedding favor. Whether it's your grandmother's famous jam recipe, hand-poured candles, homemade fudge, biscuits, or even a batch of your signature hot sauce — homemade favors carry a warmth and personality that shop-bought gifts simply can't match.

The key is in the packaging. A simple mason jar with a fabric lid cover, a kraft paper bag with a handwritten tag, or a little box tied with twine can make even the most humble homemade treat feel special. If you don't have time to make everything yourself, enlist your bridesmaids or family — it's a lovely way to involve the people closest to you in the wedding preparations.

Handmade ceramic ornaments are the kind of wedding favor that ends up on someone's Christmas tree or kitchen shelf for years. Small ceramic hearts, stars, or even miniature vases make beautiful keepsakes — especially when they're stamped or painted with your wedding date. If you're feeling creative, some couples even attend a pottery class together and make the favors themselves — which is a brilliant pre-wedding date idea in its own right.

It's the kind of favor that sparks conversation and gives guests a genuine insight into who you are as a couple.

Guests will pop them in their pocket or bag and use them for months — lighting candles at home and being reminded of your wedding every time. You can go bold with colour illustrations or keep it classic with foil-stamped monograms. Either way, they look gorgeous displayed in a bowl or scattered across the table.

They're also brilliantly lightweight and flat, which makes them easy to slip into a suitcase — ideal if your wedding is a destination celebration or elopement abroad and guests need to travel home with their favors.

## 15. Go Local

<div class="blog-photo-grid">
<img src="/images/blog-favors-local-1.jpg" alt="Silver pomegranate trinket dish wedding favor with personalised tag from Andalusia Spain" />
<img src="/images/blog-favors-local-2.jpg" alt="Tinned fish candle wedding favor on colourful ceramic plate at destination wedding table setting" />
</div>
<small>Photos via <a href="https://pinterest.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pinterest</a></small>

If you're getting married in another country, one of the best things you can do is lean into the local culture for your wedding favors. Destination weddings are all about the sense of place — so why not give your guests a piece of it to take home?

Getting married in Greece? An evil eye charm is a beautiful symbol of protection and good luck. Tying the knot in Andalusia, Spain? A silver pomegranate dish — a traditional symbol of Granada — makes a stunning keepsake that guests can use to hold spices, jewellery, or small trinkets. Saying your vows in Portugal? Consider a hand-painted ceramic sardine or even custom-designed sardine tins with your names and wedding date — they're iconic, quirky, and completely unforgettable.

The beauty of going local is that your favors tell the story of where your wedding happened. Guests don't just take home a gift — they take home a memory of the place, the culture, and the experience. And because local crafts tend to be lightweight and compact, they're easy to slip into a suitcase for the journey home.
